# UTMA/UGMA Custodial Account Recommendation

**Date:** November 20, 2025
**For:** 10-year-old son (beginning investor)

## Account Goals
- Combination of education and wealth building
- Starting with $50 initial investment
- Moderately hands-on involvement
- Investment approach: Index funds, ETFs, and individual stocks

---

## Top Recommendation: Fidelity Youth Account (UGMA/UTMA)

### Why it's ideal:
- **No account fees or minimums** - Perfect for starting with $50
- **Excellent educational tools** - Has a companion app where your son can track investments and learn
- **Flexible investment options** - Supports index funds, ETFs, and individual stocks
- **Strong fractional shares** - Can buy partial shares of expensive stocks with small amounts
- **Parent oversight** - You maintain control but he can see holdings and participate in decisions

### Key Features:
- Free stock/ETF trades
- Access to Fidelity's excellent research tools
- Can set up automatic investments
- Strong customer service and educational resources

---

## Strong Alternative: Charles Schwab UTMA/UGMA

### Why it's also excellent:
- **No minimums or fees** for custodial accounts
- **Schwab Stock Slices** - Buy fractional shares of S&P 500 stocks for as little as $5
- **Great ETF selection** - Including commission-free Schwab index funds
- **Educational resources** - Good learning materials for young investors
- **Robust platform** - Grows with your son as he becomes more sophisticated

---

---

## Next Steps

1. **Open online** - Both can be opened in 15-20 minutes
2. **You'll need:** SSN for you and your son, ID, basic info
3. **Initial investment:** Start with your $50
4. **First investments:** Consider a simple S&P 500 index fund (FXAIX at Fidelity or SWPPX at Schwab) plus maybe one stock he recognizes/likes

---

## Final Recommendation

**Start with Fidelity Youth** given your goals. The educational features are slightly better for a 10-year-old, and the fractional shares work across all stocks, giving you maximum flexibility to teach with companies he knows.
